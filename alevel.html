      13. Sample A-Level Question

      (a)(i) In an experiment to determine the volume of a cylinder, a student measured its diameter, d and height, h. The student computed the value of volume by using the expression, v = (π/8) d³ h. Deduce whether the student’s formula was dimensionally correct or not.

      (ii) If the frequency of oscillation, f, of the liquid in a U-tube depends on the acceleration due to gravity, g, the density, ρ, and height, h, of the liquid in the U-tube, use the method of dimensional analysis to derive the expression for f.

      (b) In determining the relative density of a solid, the weight of the solid in air as measured by a spring balance with accuracy of 0.01 N was 1.2 N and 0.9 N when completely immersed in water. Compute the percentage error in relative density of the solid.

      Newton's laws of motion and Projectile motion

      (a)(i) Why does a passenger apply more energy to jump from a boat to a water shore than he would need to jump the same distance between two water shores?

      (ii) A bullet of mass 15 g is fired with a speed of 600 m/s into the bob of a ballistic pendulum of mass 2.0 kg. Calculate the maximum height attained by the bob if the bullet passes through the bob and emerges with half of its original speed.

      (b) An arrow is fired horizontally at a speed of 48 m/s from a height of 1.5 m above the ground. If the arrow hits the target at a point 1.2 m above the ground, calculate:

      • (i) the time taken to hit the target.
      • (ii) the horizontal distance travelled by the arrow.
